About

Hello and welcome to my personal website! My name is Rafay, and I'm an incoming PhD student in the Department of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering at the University of Toronto, supervised by Professor Roy Kwon. I studied Engineering Science from the University of Toronto, specializing in Mathematics, Statistics and Finance. As you scroll through this website (and if you're here from my resume...), you'll quickly realize that I have a lot of software engineering and data science experience through internships and school projects. My true interests lie in understanding the financial markets through tools provided by mathematics.

I'm interested in research jobs in the asset management or data science space. My research will focus on integrating prediction and optimization problems, specifically in the financial space. I hope some of my work will generalize to further the field of mathematical programming. I would love to be an avid traveller and am taking steps to fulfill this dream. To date, I have visited 19 countries so far and made more memories and friendships than I deserve. Next trips on my list are to Morocco and Egypt, and to Colombia, Chile and Argentina.

This is a library that will allow you to integrate facial authentication into your applications easily. Easily integrates with any API, so you can use a classic REST architecture with facial recognition. Users' pictures are never moved outside the phone, for privacy reasons.

With a beautiful UI, anyone, with even the least amount of experience can utilize models for everyday use.
